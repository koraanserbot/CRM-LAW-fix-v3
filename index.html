They provide tailored solutions for each client, addressing both legal and non-legal challenges through long-term, holistic approaches.

They are deeply committed to community engagement and civic initiatives, offering pro bono representation alongside commercial cases.

Clients choose CRM Law for their compassionate, empathetic, and knowledgeable services. The firm's mission, rooted in their founding principles, aims to be more than legal representatives; they strive to be responsible professionals, mentors, volunteers, and community leaders.

John W.
Rodman

John W. Rodman is a Founding Partner at CRM LAW, LLC, with a legal career characterized by a commitment to client advocacy and community engagement. After serving honorably in the U.S. Army, where he developed valuable skills in resilience and leadership, he transitioned to law, co-founding Cox, Rodman, & Middleton, LLC in 2017. Rodman has a notable history in defending clients, having resolved over 1,000 felony cases and participated in multiple major crime jury trials. His focus on "client-centered representation" ensures that clients’ interests guide his legal strategy.

Beyond his legal work, Rodman is actively involved in community outreach. He spearheads initiatives like the "I Wish I Knew" program, which educates youth to prevent legal issues, and is a member of several local committees aimed at after-school programs and reentry services for formerly incarcerated individuals. His dedication to service illustrates his belief that legal professionals should actively contribute to their communities and be advocates for social justice.

Christopher K.
Middleton

Attorney Christopher K. Middleton, a founding partner of CRM Law, graduated cum laude from Savannah State University, where he excelled academically and athletically as a top defensive football player and student leader. He later earned his Juris Doctorate from Thurgood Marshall School of Law at Texas Southern University. Chris’s legal career began with the Public Defender’s Office, where he resolved over 1,000 cases and conducted 30+ major crime jury trials, advancing to positions like Senior Trial Team Leader and Interim Chief Public Defender.

Chris is deeply committed to community service, serving on local boards and participating in outreach efforts. He has partnered with organizations like the Savannah Bar Association and received prestigious honors like the Justice Benham Award for Community Service. Known for his leadership, professionalism, and advocacy, Chris continues to guide clients through complex legal challenges, fostering trust and achieving impactful outcomes.

Founding Partner, Falen Cox

Falen Cox, a dedicated attorney from Fairfield, Alabama, has a strong foundation in education and community service. After excelling in the Fairfield City Public School system, she attended Jacksonville State University, where she earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Criminal Justice with honors in 2007. During her time at university, she served as the parliamentarian for the Student Government Association and was recognized as the SGA Senator of the Year for two consecutive years.

Cox began her legal career at the Eastern Judicial Circuit's Public Defender's Office, advancing to the role of senior trial attorney, where she developed her litigation skills. She co-founded CRM Law, focusing on criminal defense and personal injury cases. Beyond her legal accomplishments, Cox is committed to community outreach, leading programs like Best Foot Forward, which empowers young girls, and participating in various initiatives through the Hungry Club Forum to support and uplift her community.
